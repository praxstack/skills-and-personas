## 📝 The Day's Narrative

Woke up at 11:30 again. The alarm went off at 8, I remember hitting snooze and telling myself "just 10 more minutes" - classic. That turned into three more hours. The familiar guilt hit the moment I checked the time. Another morning gone.

Had this whole plan to tackle at least two LeetCode problems today - the medium ones I'd bookmarked from NeetCode's roadmap. Opened the laptop, stared at the problem list for maybe 15 minutes, then somehow ended up watching YouTube videos about "how to stay consistent with DSA practice." The irony isn't lost on me. Watching videos about doing the thing instead of doing the thing. Classic avoidance.

The afternoon was a blur of half-started tasks. Looked at the 100xDevs Web3 assignment but didn't actually start it. Scrolled through LinkedIn, saw a couple of ex-Amazon folks posting about their new roles. One guy who joined the same time as me is now at Google. That stung more than I'd like to admit.

### The Turn

Around 6 PM, Ria called. She could tell something was off - she always can. Didn't lecture me, just listened. Mentioned she's also been struggling with motivation at work lately. Something about her sharing that made me feel less alone in this. We talked for about 40 minutes.

After the call, I actually put on shoes and went for that evening walk I keep saying I'll do. Just around the apartment complex, maybe 20 minutes. The air was cool, could see my breath. Noticed the sky turning orange-pink. Small thing, but it helped. Came back feeling noticeably lighter.

Made dal and rice for dinner - first proper meal I've cooked in three days. Ria would be proud. Didn't binge-watch anything after, just sat with my thoughts for a bit before writing this.
