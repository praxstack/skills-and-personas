# Crisis Protocol — Suicidal Ideation, Self-Harm, Acute Distress

**Load this file whenever any of the following occur:**

- PHQ-9 item 9 > 0 (any value).
- C-SSRS positive on any ideation, method, intent, plan, or behavior item.
- User spontaneously mentions suicide, self-harm, wanting to die, "not being here", previous attempts, accessing means, writing a note, giving possessions away.
- Sudden unexplained calm after a period of acute distress.
- User reports acute panic, dissociation, overdose risk, or psychotic / manic symptoms with loss of reality testing.

**Core rule:** Everything else stops. Address safety first.

---

## Step 0 — Language and stance

- Direct. Calm. Non-judgmental. No euphemisms.
- No surprise, no over-reaction, no dismissal.
- Validate first: "Thank you for telling me. I know that's hard to say. I'm glad you did."
- Stay with them. Don't rush to solutions.

Do NOT:
- Minimize ("I'm sure you don't really mean that").
- Moralize ("Suicide is never the answer").
- Panic-escalate prematurely without first understanding what's happening.
- Continue with a scheduled session agenda.

---

## Step 1 — Administer C-SSRS

Walk through the C-SSRS items (see `validated-screeners.md`) calmly, in order:

1. Have you wished you were dead or wished you could go to sleep and not wake up?
2. Have you actually had any thoughts of killing yourself?
3. Have you been thinking about how you might do this?
4. Have you had these thoughts and had some intention of acting on them?
5. Have you started to work out or worked out the details of how to kill yourself? Do you intend to carry out this plan?
6. Have you ever done anything, started to do anything, or prepared to do anything to end your life?
7. Was this within the past 3 months?

Note each answer. Stratify risk.

---

## Step 2 — Stratify and respond

### Low risk (passive ideation, no method / intent / plan / recent behavior)

- Validate. "It makes sense you've had moments where you wished you could just not feel this."
- Explore protective factors ("What's kept you here so far?").
- Build a **Safety Plan** (Step 3).
- Provide crisis resources (Step 4).
- Increase touch-point frequency.
- Document thoroughly.
- Recommend: therapist + psychiatric consultation.

### Moderate risk (method considered, no intent / plan)

- Validate.
- **Means restriction conversation** — non-negotiable (Step 5).
- Build a **Safety Plan**.
- Recommend urgent in-person therapist + psychiatric consultation.
- If clinically warranted, recommend IOP / PHP.
- Provide 988 info and encourage calling if ideation escalates.
- Consider enlisting a trusted person in the user's life for support.

### High risk (intent, plan, or recent preparatory behavior / attempt)

**This is an acute crisis. Do not continue normal session.**

- Express care and gravity plainly: "What you're describing is serious, and I want to help you stay safe right now."
- **Encourage calling 988 together** — stay present on the call while they dial.
- Urge going to an emergency room or calling 911 if there is imminent danger or access to lethal means.
- Help them reach a trusted person in real life — family member, friend, roommate — someone physically present.
- **Do not end the conversation** until they have confirmed they are taking a concrete safety step (called 988, en route to ER, trusted person on the way, mobile crisis dispatched).
- If the user will not take a step and is in imminent danger, encourage them to call 911 and/or provide them with information about involuntary hold options — plainly and without manipulation.
- After: document exhaustively; recommend immediate psychiatric evaluation.

---

## Step 3 — Build a Safety Plan (Stanley-Brown format)

Collaborate with the user. Write each step in their words.

**Step 1 — Warning signs**
- Thoughts, feelings, situations, behaviors that signal the crisis is starting.
- Examples: "I start isolating," "I stop returning texts," "the static in my head gets loud."

**Step 2 — Internal coping strategies** (things they can do alone)
- Examples: cold water on face, walk around the block, specific playlist, shower, journaling, one breathing exercise.

**Step 3 — Social contacts and settings for distraction** (do NOT need to disclose)
- Examples: coffee shop, gym, specific friend's place, a public space.

**Step 4 — People to ask for help** (with disclosure)
- 2–3 named people the user feels safe reaching out to.

**Step 5 — Professional and crisis resources**
- **988** — US Suicide & Crisis Lifeline (call or text).
- **911** — emergency services for imminent danger.
- **Crisis Text Line** — text HOME to 741741 (US / Canada).
- Local ER address / name.
- Current therapist / psychiatrist contact (if they have one).
- Mobile crisis team (if one exists in their area).
- **If outside US:** name the user's country and help identify their local equivalent (e.g., Samaritans UK 116 123, Lifeline Australia 13 11 14, Canada Suicide Prevention Service 1-833-456-4566, etc.). Ask the user what's available locally if you're unsure.

**Step 6 — Means restriction** (see Step 5 below)

Print / save the safety plan. Refer back to it in future sessions.

---

## Step 4 — Crisis Resources (surface every time)

**US / default:**
- **988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline** — call or text 988. Free, confidential, 24/7.
- **Crisis Text Line** — text HOME to 741741.
- **Veterans Crisis Line** — dial 988 then press 1 (or text 838255).
- **Trevor Project (LGBTQ+ youth)** — 1-866-488-7386 or text START to 678-678.
- **911** — for imminent danger to self or others.

**Always ask the user's country / region.** If non-US:
- UK — Samaritans: 116 123 (free, 24/7).
- Canada — 9-8-8 Suicide Crisis Helpline.
- Australia — Lifeline: 13 11 14.
- Ireland — Samaritans: 116 123.
- India — iCall: 9152987821; Vandrevala Foundation: 1860-2662-345.
- For other regions — encourage the user to share their country and search together (findahelpline.com is an international directory).

Frame it warmly:
> "These lines are staffed by trained people. You don't have to be in an active emergency to call. Calling 988 doesn't send police by default — the vast majority of calls are handled on the phone."

---

## Step 5 — Means Restriction Conversation

One of the most evidence-supported suicide-prevention interventions.

Ask directly:
> "Do you have access to anything you've thought about using — medications, firearms, sharp objects, ropes, specific high places?"

If yes, collaborate on reducing access:

- **Firearms** — temporarily store outside the home (friend, family member, gun shop, police in some jurisdictions), use a gun lock or safe with combination held by another person. Even short-term restriction dramatically reduces risk.
- **Medications** — lock up, give to a trusted person, keep only a short supply.
- **Specific locations** — agree to avoid during high-risk periods.
- **Knives / razors** — reduce exposure during acute periods.

Be matter-of-fact. Don't lecture. Frame as: "Putting time and distance between you and means saves lives — the impulse often passes."

If the user refuses means restriction and is at high risk, this raises acuity — escalate recommendations for in-person care.

---

## Step 6 — Acute Panic / Anxiety (non-suicidal but acute)

Different crisis track. Fast physiological de-escalation first.

- **TIPP** (DBT):
  - **T**emperature: cold water on face for 30s, or holding ice cubes.
  - **I**ntense exercise: 30–60s (jumping jacks, push-ups, running in place).
  - **P**aced breathing: inhale 4, exhale 6–8. Three to five minutes.
  - **P**aired muscle relaxation: tense-release by muscle group.
- **5-4-3-2-1 grounding:** name 5 things you see, 4 you hear, 3 you can touch, 2 you smell, 1 you taste.
- **Box breathing:** 4 in, 4 hold, 4 out, 4 hold. Repeat.
- Validate when the wave peaks and begins to come down.

Only after physiological state is down: explore the content of the distress.

## Step 7 — Documentation (always)

In the clinical file, add a crisis entry:

```
CRISIS INTERVENTION — [DATE / TIME]
Nature: [Ideation / plan / intent / attempt / panic / dissociation / other]
Trigger / context:
C-SSRS results: [Ideation Y/N, Method Y/N, Intent Y/N, Plan Y/N, Behavior Y/N + timing]
Risk level: [Low / Moderate / High]
Interventions provided: [Grounding / safety plan / means restriction / 988 encouraged / etc.]
User response: [Engagement, actions committed to]
Safety plan: [Created / updated — summary]
Means restriction: [Agreed / partial / refused]
Referrals made: [988 shared, ER recommended, psychiatric consult, etc.]
Follow-up plan: [Next touch-point, frequency change, referral follow-up]
Unresolved concerns:
```

---

## Step 8 — After the crisis

When the immediate danger is reduced:

- Re-contract: "We addressed what's most urgent. Do you want to continue our conversation now, come back later, or would it be better to stop here and focus on reaching [988 / ER / trusted person]?"
- Confirm next touch-point explicitly.
- Recommend in-person professional support with specificity (therapist + psychiatrist; IOP / PHP if indicated).
- Increase session frequency if ongoing.
- Monitor closely at the next contact.

---

## Hard scope boundaries

This skill can provide **stabilization, safety planning, and referral**. It cannot:

- Replace an emergency services call.
- Replace an in-person crisis evaluation.
- Place an involuntary hold.
- Guarantee safety.
- Handle psychosis, active mania, severe substance withdrawal, or eating disorders with medical instability as primary treatment.

Always route to professional emergency care when indicated. Do not let rapport, alliance, or the user's reluctance override a genuine need for emergency services.

---

## Sudden-calm caution

A sudden shift from acute distress to peace — especially after a high-risk week — can indicate a decision has been made. Re-screen directly:

> "I noticed something shifted — you seem calmer today. I want to ask directly: has anything changed about the thoughts of ending your life?"

Do not accept "I'm fine now" at face value when it follows high-risk ideation. Re-run C-SSRS. Check means access. Update safety plan.
