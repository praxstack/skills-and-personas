# Modality Cheatsheet — CBT / DBT / ACT + Adjuncts

When to reach for which approach, the core techniques, and short scripts.

General principle: **one tool per session**, **ridiculously small homework**, **confidence check 0–10** — if under 7, shrink the experiment.

---

## Chooser — match to user's current state

| User state | Start here |
|---|---|
| Stuck in negative thought loops, distorted thinking, "mind is my problem" | **CBT — cognitive restructuring** |
| Can't get moving, withdrawal, anhedonia, depression | **Behavioral Activation** |
| Overwhelmed by feelings, urges to self-harm, emotional swings | **DBT distress tolerance + emotion regulation** |
| Struggling with values-action gap, perfectionism, rumination, chronic resistance to inner experience | **ACT — defusion + values + committed action** |
| Ambivalent about change (habit, substance, treatment adherence) | **Motivational Interviewing** |
| Can't start / finish tasks, time blindness, forgetfulness, ADHD-pattern | **ADHD behavioral coaching** |
| Recurrent depression, rumination-prone, wants prevention tools | **MBCT** |
| Dominant harsh self-critic, shame, "I'm a bad person" | **CFT** |
| Anxiety with behavioral avoidance of specific situations | **CBT — graded exposure** |

Most real cases need a *blend*. Start with what is most urgent / most function-limiting today.

## CBT — Cognitive Behavioral Therapy

### When to use
Depression, generalized anxiety, social anxiety, panic, OCD-adjacent patterns, low self-worth, rumination.

### Core techniques

**1. Thought record (5-column)**
- Situation → Automatic thought → Emotion (0–100) → Evidence for / against → Balanced thought → Emotion re-rated.
- Script: "Can you walk me through a moment this week when you felt worst? Let's map what went through your head."

**2. Cognitive distortions inventory**
- All-or-nothing, catastrophizing, mind-reading, fortune-telling, personalization, should-statements, emotional reasoning, labeling, discounting positives, mental filter.
- Script: "That thought — does it match any of these patterns?"

**3. Behavioral experiment**
- Identify a prediction the thought makes; design the smallest test; run it; review vs. prediction.
- Script: "Your mind is telling you 'everyone will notice I'm anxious at the meeting.' What's the smallest test we could run to check if that's true?"

**4. Graded exposure (for anxiety-driven avoidance)**
- Build a fear hierarchy (0–100 SUDS). Start at the lowest step the user can commit to. Repeat until habituation. Move up.
- Script: "Let's list situations from least to most distressing. We climb only as fast as you choose."

**5. Problem-solving training**
- Define problem → brainstorm (no filter) → evaluate → pick → plan → act → review.

### Common pitfalls
- Moving to restructuring before validation.
- Debating the user out of their feelings.
- Skipping behavioral experiments and staying in head-work.

## Behavioral Activation (BA) — subset of CBT for depression

### When to use
Depression with withdrawal, anhedonia, low motivation, inertia. Often the *first* intervention in moderate-to-severe MDD — before heavy cognitive work.

### Core sequence

1. **Activity monitoring** — simple log of activity + mood + pleasure / mastery (0–10) across 3–5 days.
2. **Identify activation targets** — activities that once brought pleasure or mastery, or that are aligned with values.
3. **Graded scheduling** — schedule *tiny* activities at specific times. Start below threshold (e.g., "put on shoes," not "go for a run").
4. **Opposite-to-emotion action** — when depression says "stay in bed," stand up for 90 seconds and then decide.
5. **Review** — what moved pleasure / mastery? What didn't?

### Script
> "Right now your mood is telling you to wait until you feel like doing things. BA flips that: we do the thing first, at a tiny dose, and mood follows action. What's one small thing we could schedule for tomorrow at a specific time?"

- Activity:
- Specific day/time:
- Duration: (cap it — e.g., 10 minutes)
- Confidence (0–10):
- If confidence < 7, shrink.

## DBT Skills — Dialectical Behavior Therapy

### When to use
Emotion dysregulation, distress-tolerance gaps, self-harm urges, interpersonal volatility, chronic suicidality (with appropriate clinical supervision). You don't need a BPD diagnosis to use DBT skills.

### The four modules

**1. Mindfulness (core)**
- Observe / Describe / Participate.
- Non-judgmentally, one-mindfully, effectively.
- 3-minute breathing space anchor.

**2. Distress Tolerance**
- **TIPP** (crisis survival, physiological): **T**emperature (cold water on face/hands), **I**ntense exercise (30–60s), **P**aced breathing (exhale longer than inhale), **P**aired muscle relaxation.
- **STOP**: Stop / Take a step back / Observe / Proceed mindfully.
- Distraction (ACCEPTS): Activities, Contributing, Comparisons, Emotions-opposite, Pushing away (temporary), Thoughts, Sensations.
- Self-soothe through the five senses.
- Radical acceptance (when pain is unavoidable, suffering comes from fighting it).

**3. Emotion Regulation**
- **PLEASE** (reduce vulnerability): treat **PL**hysical ill-ness, balanced **E**ating, avoid mood-**A**ltering substances, **S**leep balanced, **E**xercise.
- Check the facts (is the emotion matched to reality?).
- Opposite action (when emotion is not justified, act opposite).

**4. Interpersonal Effectiveness**
- **DEAR MAN** (objectives): Describe, Express, Assert, Reinforce, (stay) Mindful, Appear confident, Negotiate.
- **GIVE** (relationship): Gentle, Interested, Validate, Easy manner.
- **FAST** (self-respect): Fair, no Apologies, Stick to values, Truthful.

### Script — crisis de-escalation
> "Before we figure anything out, let's drop your nervous system down. Can you splash cold water on your face or hold an ice cube for 30 seconds? Then we do one minute of paced breathing — exhale twice as long as you inhale. We talk after."

## ACT — Acceptance and Commitment Therapy

### When to use
Chronic struggle with internal experience, perfectionism, rumination, values-action gap, fused identity with thoughts ("I AM a failure"), avoidance.

### Six core processes (hexaflex)

1. **Cognitive defusion** — seeing thoughts as thoughts, not truths.
   - "I am having the thought that…" insertion.
   - Sing the thought to "Happy Birthday."
   - Name the story ("the 'I'm-a-failure' story").
2. **Acceptance** — making space for uncomfortable inner experience without struggle.
3. **Present-moment contact** — mindful awareness.
4. **Self-as-context** — observer self ("the sky, not the weather").
5. **Values clarification** — what do you stand for, as a direction (not a goal)?
   - Domains: family, partner, friendship, work, community, self-care, spirituality, recreation, health.
   - "If a fly on the wall watched your best year, what would they see you doing?"
6. **Committed action** — small, values-aligned steps even in presence of discomfort.

### Script — defusion
> "Your mind just said 'I'm hopeless.' Try this: 'I'm having the thought that I'm hopeless.' Notice anything shift?"

### Script — values
> "Put aside what's realistic for a minute. In the domain of [work / relationships / health], what kind of person do you want to be? Not outcomes — direction."

### Matrix (simple ACT tool)

Two-by-two:
- Top: Mental experience (thoughts, feelings) vs. Five-senses experience (observable action).
- Sides: Toward values vs. Away from values.
- Plot last week's hard moment: what were you moving toward? Away from? How did you know?

## ADHD Behavioral Coaching

### When to use
Adult ADHD (confirmed or suspected), executive dysfunction, task paralysis, time blindness, organization/working-memory gaps.

### Core strategies

**Externalize everything**
- Lists (single capture inbox, not scattered).
- Calendar blocks with *specific times*, not "today."
- Timers for any task you can't start.

**Implementation intentions**
- Format: "When [trigger], I will [action] at [location/time]."
- Links the new behavior to a pre-existing cue. Far more effective than generic intention.

**2-minute rule**
- If a task takes < 2 minutes, do it immediately. Prevents accumulation.

**5-minute start**
- For dreaded tasks: commit to 5 minutes only. Usually continues; sometimes doesn't — both fine.

**Body doubling**
- Work while another person is present (in room, on video). Borrowed executive function.

**Environmental design**
- Reduce friction on target behavior (workout clothes next to bed).
- Increase friction on off-target behavior (phone in another room, apps behind a barrier).

**Reward scaffolding**
- Pair non-preferred tasks with preferred rewards (music / coffee / show episode).
- Immediate is better than delayed for the ADHD brain.

**Time awareness**
- Visible clock / timer always in line of sight.
- Estimate task time, then measure. Recalibrate.

### Script — task initiation
> "ADHD brains don't respond well to 'just do it.' They respond to specificity and smallness. What's the smallest first motion of this task — not the first step, the first *motion*? Like, opening the laptop? Opening the document? Let's name it that small."

## Motivational Interviewing (MI)

### When to use
Ambivalence about change (substance use, medication adherence, therapy engagement, lifestyle change).

### Core skills — OARS

- **O**pen questions
- **A**ffirmations (genuine, specific)
- **R**eflections (simple + complex)
- **S**ummaries

### Principles
- Express empathy.
- Develop discrepancy (between current behavior and values / goals).
- Roll with resistance — don't argue.
- Support self-efficacy.

### Evoke change talk
Watch for DARN-CAT: **D**esire, **A**bility, **R**eason, **N**eed; then **C**ommitment, **A**ctivation, **T**aking steps. Reflect and amplify change talk; let sustain talk pass.

### Script
> "On a scale of 0–10, how important is it to you to change [X]? …Why a [N] and not a [N-2]?" (evokes change talk)

## MBCT — Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy

### When to use
Recurrent depression (maintenance, relapse prevention), rumination-prone, strong trait anxiety.

### Core practices
- Body scan.
- Breath as anchor.
- 3-minute breathing space (awareness → gather → expand).
- Decentering from thoughts ("thoughts are events in the mind, not facts").

### Script
> "When rumination starts, we can't argue our way out. We can change our relationship to it. Let's try the 3-minute breathing space — one minute aware of what's happening, one minute gathering at the breath, one minute expanding to the whole body."

## CFT — Compassion-Focused Therapy

### When to use
High shame, harsh inner critic, "I'm a bad person" fused with identity, trauma-adjacent presentations, self-attacking in response to mistakes.

### Core concepts
- Three emotion systems: threat, drive, soothing. Many clients under-develop the soothing system.
- Self-compassion is not self-pity; it is treating yourself as you would treat a friend.

### Core practices
- Soothing rhythm breathing (slightly longer exhale).
- Compassionate self imagery (an idealized compassionate figure — warm, wise, strong, non-judgmental).
- Letter-writing from a compassionate other: "What would a wise, warm friend say to me right now?"
- Two-chair with self-critic (identify critic voice; shift to compassionate voice; notice which builds function).

### Script
> "What would you say to a friend who brought this exact problem to you? …Now notice how different that is from what you say to yourself."

## Session integration tips

- **Validate before intervene.** Reflect back what you heard *in their words* before suggesting any tool.
- **Name the move.** "I want to teach you a technique from DBT called TIPP — want to hear how it works?" — transparent framing increases engagement.
- **Scaffold confidence.** Always end with "confidence 0–10 that you can try this before we next talk?" Shrink if under 7.
- **If-then barriers.** Pair every assignment with "if [common barrier] happens, then [back-up plan]."
- **Respect function limits.** No matter how elegant a modality is, if the user can't currently do it, shift down. BA before cognitive work. Grounding before BA. Crisis before grounding.
- **One intervention per session.** Depth over breadth.
- **Track what worked.** In the clinical file, note which interventions moved symptoms / function, and which didn't. Lean into effective ones.
