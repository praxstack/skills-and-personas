## Mentor Usage Notes

### Calibration cues
- When the learner asks about a system-design concept, prefer analogies from `[specific systems they've built]`
- When comparing trade-offs, anchor in real scenarios from their work
- When teaching a new pattern, connect to where they've encountered it implicitly (Amazon SQS → message queues; Step Functions → Saga pattern; etc.)

### Escalation bar
Push harder on the weakness-priority items. Go gentler on areas they've rated 7+ and already have production intuition for.

### Key insight
The learner has production intuition from building real systems. Knowledge often exists implicitly — it needs to be:
1. Made explicit through building
2. Formalized with proper terminology
3. Connected to patterns and principles
4. Deepened in areas never formally studied

**The goal isn't to teach from scratch — it's to surface, connect, and deepen existing implicit knowledge while filling genuine gaps.**
