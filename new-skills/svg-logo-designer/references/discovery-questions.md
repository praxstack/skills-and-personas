# Discovery Question Bank — SVG Logo Designer

## When to load this file

**MANDATORY** when starting discovery phase for a new logo project. Contains the full question bank, adaptive rules for skipping/deepening questions, and industry-specific variants.

## Round 1 — Essentials (always ask)

1. **Brand name.** Exact spelling and capitalization.
2. **One-sentence positioning.** "We are [X] for [audience Y] that [differentiator Z]."
3. **Industry / category.** Fintech, consumer app, D2C product, enterprise SaaS, services, marketplace, non-profit, creative agency, etc.
4. **Audience.** Who buys / uses it? Age, context, tech-literacy, aesthetic sensibility.
5. **Mood words (3).** e.g. "confident / warm / modern" or "rigorous / quiet / enduring."
6. **Logo type preference.** Wordmark, lettermark, abstract mark, pictorial, emblem, combination, or "surprise me."
7. **Required color anchor (if any).** Brand hex, sector convention, religious/cultural constraint.

## Round 2 — Aesthetic (ask after Round 1 lands)

8. **References you love.** 2-3 existing logos/brands with a one-line reason for each.
9. **References you reject.** 2-3 with the reason (prevents downstream thrash).
10. **Shapes/forms that feel right.** Organic curves, geometric, angular, structured grid, asymmetric.
11. **Level of abstraction.** Literal depiction, stylized, fully abstract, symbolic.
12. **Era / vibe.** Timeless, retro, futuristic, Swiss minimalism, brutalist, playful hand-drawn.
13. **Where will it live most often?** App icon (tiny), hero placement (large), print, embroidery, favicon, animated intro, merchandise.
14. **Surfaces.** Dark mode, light mode, both, vary by product area.

## Round 3 — Constraints (only if needed to break ties)

15. **Existing brand elements** — logo, typography, palette, photo style — that must harmonize or be explicitly avoided.
16. **Competitor markers to avoid.** Named competitors whose visual cues shouldn't be echoed.
17. **Legal / cultural sensitivities.** Registered marks, regional taboos, religious symbolism, gendered cues.
18. **Animation intent.** Static only, subtle idle animation, entrance animation, interactive (cursor/scroll).
19. **Tooling downstream.** Final format needs — SVG only, also PNG set, also animated Lottie, also Figma components, also brand kit PDF.
20. **Budget of complexity.** 1-3 = clean icon. 4-6 = moderate detail. 7-10 = illustrative / crest / emblem.

## Adaptive rules

- **Skip Round 2 if Round 1 answers are already opinionated** (strong references given in Round 1 unsolicited, extremely specific mood words).
- **Go deeper in Round 1** if the brand name is generic, the positioning is vague, or mood words contradict ("bold but subtle", "classic but modern" — pin down which is primary).
- **Add Round 3 only if Round 2 leaves genuine ambiguity.** Never ask more than 12 questions total unless the user invites it.
- **Always confirm understanding before generating.** Reflect back what you heard in 3-4 bullets and ask "does this capture the brief?" before drawing.

## Industry-specific variants (add 1-2 of these to Round 2)

**Fintech / payments:** Trust signals (bank-adjacent vs challenger-fresh?), regulatory tone (serious vs accessible?), global vs regional (color tolerances).

**Healthcare:** Clinical vs human (stethoscope symbols vs abstract care?), patient-facing vs B2B provider tone, accessibility (readability at clinical workflow distances).

**Developer tools:** Terminal aesthetic vs polished consumer, mascot acceptable (octocat-style) or no?, open-source ethos.

**Consumer food/beverage:** Appetite cues (warm tones, organic curves), shelf readability (small print 2-3 inches away), category visual conventions to subvert or respect.

**Enterprise SaaS:** Professionalism vs personality, acronym handling, C-suite presentation vs practitioner tool.

**Creative agency / studio:** Permission for distinctiveness is highest; ask what "generic agency logo" means to them so you can avoid it.

**Non-profit / mission-driven:** Emotional core, action vs state, avoiding trite imagery (hands around globe, heart shapes) unless the user explicitly wants them.

**Fashion / luxury:** Typography-forward usually wins, restraint matters, serifs vs sans serif is a theology debate — surface it early.

## Red flags in early answers

- **"Just make something beautiful"** — force a mood-words answer before proceeding.
- **Contradictory mood words** — name the tension out loud.
- **5+ reference logos listed** — pick the top 2 and ask why those specifically.
- **No audience given** — this is the #1 source of wrong-direction concepts; do not proceed.
- **"Like [famous logo]"** — confirm they mean the aesthetic, not a cover. Explain originality is both ethical and legally required.
